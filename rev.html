Revolutionary Activites

Youth Activism and Formation of Naujawan Bharat Sabha

Bhagat Singh became politically active at a very young age, and he founded the Naujawan Bharat Sabha in 1926 along with other like-minded youth leaders. The organization aimed to bring together young people who were disillusioned with the Indian National Congress and were looking for more radical ways to fight for India's freedom.

Participation in the Non-Cooperation Movement

Bhagat Singh was an active participant in the Non-Cooperation Movement launched by Mahatma Gandhi in 1920. However, he became disillusioned with the movement's non-violent methods and started advocating for more aggressive and direct action to overthrow British colonial rule.

Bombing of the Central Legislative Assembly

In 1928, Bhagat Singh and his fellow revolutionaries bombed the Central Legislative Assembly in Delhi as a protest against the Public Safety Bill and the Trade Disputes Bill. The bombing was meant to send a message to the British government that they were no longer going to tolerate the injustices of colonial rule.

Hunger Strike in Jail

After the bombing of the Central Legislative Assembly, Bhagat Singh and his colleagues were arrested and imprisoned. In jail, they went on a hunger strike to protest against the inhumane treatment of political prisoners. The hunger strike lasted for 116 days, and it drew international attention to the Indian independence movement.

Assassination of British Police Officer

In 1928, Bhagat Singh and his associates also assassinated a British police officer named John Saunders in Lahore. The assassination was a retaliation for the death of Lala Lajpat Rai, a prominent Indian nationalist leader who died after being beaten by the police during a protest.

Trial and Execution

Bhagat Singh and his associates were put on trial for their revolutionary activities, and they used the court proceedings as an opportunity to bring attention to the injustices of British colonial rule. Despite their efforts, they were found guilty and sentenced to death. Bhagat Singh, Rajguru, and Sukhdev were executed on March 23, 1931.
